Pension Claims After Death (India):

OVERVIEW:
When a pensioner passes away, the spouse and dependents may be eligible for family pension. The process varies based on whether the deceased was a government employee, private sector employee (EPS/NPS), or a military/defense personnel.

TYPE 1: GOVERNMENT PENSION (Central/State Government Employees)

FAMILY PENSION ELIGIBILITY:
- Spouse (widow/widower) — until death or remarriage
- Unmarried/divorced/widowed daughters — until marriage/remarriage or employment
- Sons — until age 25 or employment
- Disabled children — for lifetime

STEP 1: Inform the Pension Disbursing Authority
  - For Central Govt: Treasury Office / Bank branch where pension is credited
  - For State Govt: State Treasury / Directorate of Pensions

STEP 2: Submit Required Documents
  - Death certificate (certified copy)
  - PPO (Pension Payment Order) number
  - Family pension application form
  - Spouse/dependent's identity proof (Aadhaar + PAN)
  - Spouse/dependent's bank account details
  - Marriage certificate (for spouse)
  - Birth certificate of dependent children (if applicable)
  - Joint photo of deceased and spouse (if available)
  - Life certificate (Jeevan Pramaan) of the claimant

STEP 3: Apply for Family Pension
  - Central Govt: Through CPAO (Central Pension Accounting Office)
    Link: https://cpao.nic.in/
  - Defence: Through PCDA (Principal Controller of Defence Accounts)
    Link: https://pcdapension.nic.in/
  - State Govt: Through respective State Pension Department
  - CCS (Pension) Rules, 2021 applicable for Central Govt
    Link: https://doppw.gov.in/

STEP 4: Processing
  - Family pension should commence from the day after the pensioner's death
  - Enhanced family pension: 50% of last pay for first 7 years or up to age 67, whichever is earlier
  - Normal family pension: 30% of last pay thereafter
  - Timeline: 1-3 months for processing

STEP 5: Arrears and Gratuity
  - Any pending pension arrears will be paid to the family
  - Death Gratuity: Payable if death occurs while in service (up to 16.5 times of pay, max ₹20 lakh)

TYPE 2: EPS PENSION (Employees' Pension Scheme — Private Sector)

ELIGIBILITY:
- Spouse and up to 2 children of deceased EPF member
- Member must have completed at least 10 years of service

FAMILY PENSION:
- Widow pension: Based on service length and average salary
- Children's pension: Additional 25% of widow pension per child (max 2)
- Orphan pension: 75% of widow pension per child

STEP 1: Submit Form 10D to EPFO
  - Download: https://www.epfindia.gov.in/site_en/Forms.php
STEP 2: Required Documents:
  - Form 10D (duly filled)
  - Death certificate
  - Marriage certificate
  - Spouse's Aadhaar and bank details
  - UAN of deceased member
  - Employer attestation (if available)
STEP 3: Submit to regional EPFO office or online via member portal
STEP 4: Processing: 20-30 working days
  Link: https://unifiedportal-mem.epfindia.gov.in/

TYPE 3: NPS (National Pension System)

IF SUBSCRIBER DIES BEFORE RETIREMENT:
- Entire accumulated corpus paid to nominee/legal heir as lump sum
- No mandatory annuity purchase required

IF SUBSCRIBER DIES AFTER RETIREMENT:
- Depends on annuity type chosen:
  - If "Return of Purchase Price" annuity: Lump sum returned to nominee
  - If "Joint Life" annuity: Pension continues to spouse

STEP 1: Nominee/heir submits claim to NPS Point of Presence (POP)
  - Or through employer (for government NPS subscribers)
STEP 2: Documents:
  - Death certificate
  - PRAN (Permanent Retirement Account Number) card
  - Nominee's/heir's KYC
  - Bank account details
STEP 3: CRA (Central Recordkeeping Agency) processes the claim
  Link: https://www.npscra.nsdl.co.in/
  NSDL CRA: https://cra-nsdl.com/
STEP 4: Processing: 15-30 working days

TYPE 4: MILITARY / DEFENCE PENSION

- Contact Unit Record Office or Zila Sainik Board
- Family pension under CCS (Pension) Rules or Defence Pension Rules
- Additional benefits: Canteen facilities, medical coverage (ECHS)
- ECHS: https://echs.gov.in/
- SPARSH Portal: https://sparsh.defencepension.gov.in/

IMPORTANT NOTES:
- Family pension is a RIGHT, not a favor. Do not accept delays.
- If pension is credited to joint bank account with survivor mode: Pension continues automatically until the bank is informed about the death. Inform promptly to avoid recovery.
- Jeevan Pramaan (Digital Life Certificate) can be submitted online: https://jeevanpramaan.gov.in/
- Commuted portion of pension stands restored after 15 years from commutation.

ESCALATION:
- Central Govt: Department of Pension & Pensioners' Welfare
  Helpline: 1800-11-1960
  Link: https://doppw.gov.in/
- Pensioners' Grievance: https://pgportal.gov.in/
- If bank delays family pension payment: Approach bank nodal officer or Banking Ombudsman
