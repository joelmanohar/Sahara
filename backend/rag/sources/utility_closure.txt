Utility Account Closure / Transfer After Death (India):

OVERVIEW:
Utility accounts (electricity, water, gas, telephone, internet) in the deceased's name should be either transferred to a family member or closed to avoid accumulating bills and penalties.

ELECTRICITY CONNECTION:

STEP 1: Gather documents
  - Death certificate
  - Last paid electricity bill
  - Legal Heir Certificate or relationship proof
  - Identity proof of new connection holder
  - Property ownership/occupancy proof

STEP 2: Visit local electricity office or apply online
  - BSES (Delhi): https://www.bsesdelhi.com/
  - TATA Power (Mumbai): https://www.tatapower.com/
  - BESCOM (Bangalore): https://bescom.karnataka.gov.in/
  - TNEB (Tamil Nadu): https://www.tnebnet.org/
  - MSEDCL (Maharashtra): https://www.mahadiscom.in/

STEP 3: Submit name transfer application with documents
STEP 4: Pay any outstanding dues
STEP 5: New connection issued in heir's name (existing meter retained)
- Timeline: 7-15 working days
- If closing: Request disconnection and final bill settlement

WATER CONNECTION:

STEP 1: Visit local Municipal Corporation water department
STEP 2: Submit:
  - Death certificate
  - Identity proof of applicant
  - Property proof
  - Transfer application form
STEP 3: Pay pending water charges
STEP 4: Connection transferred or new connection issued
- Timeline: 7-21 working days

GAS CONNECTION (LPG):

STEP 1: Visit the nearest distributor (Indane/HP/Bharat Gas)
STEP 2: Submit:
  - Death certificate
  - Heir's Aadhaar and PAN
  - Address proof
  - Transfer application (Distributor Form 17)
  - Ration card showing heir as family member
STEP 3: Distributor processes transfer
STEP 4: Subsidy account linked to heir's bank (for DBTL)
- Timeline: 7-15 working days
- Indane: https://www.iocl.com/indane
- HP Gas: https://www.hindustanpetroleum.com/hpgas
- Bharat Gas: https://www.bharatgas.com/

PIPED NATURAL GAS (PNG):
- Contact the gas distribution company
  - IGL (Delhi): https://www.iglonline.net/
  - MGL (Mumbai): https://www.mahanagargas.com/
  - GAIL Gas: https://gailgas.com/
- Submit death certificate + heir's KYC + property proof
- Timeline: 7-15 working days

TELEPHONE / LANDLINE:

STEP 1: Visit BSNL/MTNL/Airtel/Jio office
STEP 2: Submit:
  - Death certificate
  - Identity proof of heir
  - Request for name transfer or disconnection
STEP 3: Settle outstanding bills
STEP 4: Transfer processed or line disconnected
- BSNL: https://portal.bsnl.in/
- Timeline: 7-15 working days
- If disconnecting: Return any equipment (modem, set-top box)

INTERNET / BROADBAND:

STEP 1: Contact the ISP (Internet Service Provider)
  - Airtel: https://www.airtel.in/
  - Jio Fiber: https://www.jio.com/fiber
  - ACT Fibernet: https://www.actcorp.in/
  - BSNL: https://portal.bsnl.in/
STEP 2: Request closure or name transfer
STEP 3: Submit death certificate + KYC
STEP 4: Return any rented equipment (router, ONT)
STEP 5: Settle final bill and get security deposit refund
- Timeline: 7-15 working days

MOBILE PHONE CONNECTION:

STEP 1: Visit the service provider's store
STEP 2: Options:
  a) Transfer to heir: Submit death certificate + heir's Aadhaar + application
  b) Close permanently: Submit death certificate + surrender form
STEP 3: Retrieve important data (contacts, photos) before closure
STEP 4: If postpaid: Settle outstanding bills
STEP 5: SIM deactivated or ported
- Important: If the mobile number is linked to bank accounts/UPI, update the registered mobile number with those institutions FIRST before closing the SIM.

CABLE TV / DTH:

STEP 1: Contact the DTH provider
  - Tata Play: https://www.tataplay.com/
  - Airtel Digital TV: https://www.airtel.in/digital-tv
  - Dish TV: https://www.dishtv.in/
  - Sun Direct: https://www.sundirect.in/
STEP 2: Request cancellation or transfer
STEP 3: Submit death certificate
STEP 4: Return set-top box for security deposit refund
- Timeline: 15-30 days for refund

GENERAL TIPS:
- Prioritize utilities linked to the property the family will continue using
- Transfer is generally easier and cheaper than closing and getting a new connection
- Always settle outstanding dues to avoid penalties or legal notices
- Keep copies of all cancellation/transfer acknowledgments
- Check for security deposits — many utility companies hold deposits that can be refunded
