Death Certificate Process (India):

OVERVIEW:
A death certificate is the most critical document required for all post-death administrative tasks. It is legally required for bank claims, insurance, property transfer, pension, and government benefits. Without it, almost no financial or legal process can begin.

STEP 1: Obtain Medical Certificate of Cause of Death (MCCD)
- If death occurred in a hospital: The attending doctor issues the MCCD automatically.
- If death occurred at home: Call the local municipal authority or nearest government hospital. A registered medical practitioner must certify the cause of death.
- If death occurred due to accident/unnatural causes: File an FIR with the police. The police will arrange post-mortem, and the hospital will issue the MCCD after post-mortem.
- Required: Identity proof of deceased, hospital records (if any)
- Timeline: Usually same day or next day

STEP 2: Register the Death
- Registration must be done within 21 days of death at the local municipal corporation / gram panchayat office.
- Late registration (after 21 days but within 30 days): Allowed with a late fee.
- Late registration (after 30 days): Requires a Magistrate's order.
- WHO registers: The head of the household, nearest relative, or hospital authority (if institutional death).
- Portal for online registration (varies by state):
  - Delhi: https://edistrict.delhigovt.nic.in/
  - Maharashtra: https://aaplesarkar.mahaonline.gov.in/
  - Karnataka: https://kaveri2.karnataka.gov.in/
  - Tamil Nadu: https://cnr.tn.gov.in/
  - General: Check your state's e-District portal

STEP 3: Required Documents for Registration
- MCCD (Medical Certificate of Cause of Death)
- Proof of identity of deceased (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport)
- Proof of identity of informant/applicant
- Proof of address of place of death
- Hospital discharge summary (if applicable)
- FIR copy (if unnatural death)

STEP 4: Obtain Death Certificate
- After registration, the Registrar issues the Death Certificate.
- Processing time: 7-15 working days (varies by state and municipality)
- Collect multiple certified copies (at least 8-10 copies recommended)
- Each copy may cost ₹10-50 depending on the state
- Online application available in many states through e-District portals

STEP 5: Certified Copies
- Get at least 8-10 certified copies — you will need them for:
  - Each bank account claim
  - Insurance claims
  - EPF/PF withdrawal
  - Property mutation
  - Pension claims
  - Utility account closure
  - Vehicle transfer
  - Court proceedings (if any)

IMPORTANT NOTES:
- If the death occurred abroad: The Indian Embassy/Consulate will assist. The foreign death certificate must be apostilled and translated.
- If no MCCD is available: A declaration by the head of the family and two witnesses may be accepted in some states.
- Keep the original death certificate safe. Always submit certified copies, not the original.
- Some institutions accept self-attested photocopies; others require notarized or gazetted officer-attested copies.

ESCALATION:
- If the municipal office delays beyond 30 days: File an RTI application or approach the District Registrar.
- For grievances: Contact the Chief Registrar of Births and Deaths in your state.
- Online grievance portals are available in most states.

TIMELINE SUMMARY:
- MCCD: 1-2 days
- Registration: Within 21 days (free), 21-30 days (with fee), after 30 days (Magistrate order)
- Death Certificate issuance: 7-15 working days after registration
- Recommended: Start within 3-5 days of death
