Nominee vs Legal Heir — Understanding the Difference (India):

OVERVIEW:
One of the most common confusions in post-death processes is the difference between a nominee and a legal heir. Understanding this distinction is critical for claiming assets effectively.

NOMINEE:
- A nominee is a person designated by the account/policy holder to receive the proceeds upon death.
- The nominee acts as a CUSTODIAN or TRUSTEE, not necessarily the legal owner.
- Supreme Court ruling (Sarbati Devi vs Usha Devi, 1984): A nominee holds the assets in trust for the legal heirs unless the nominee IS the legal heir.
- Nomination simplifies the transfer process but does NOT override a will or succession law.
- Banks, insurance companies, and mutual funds are legally required to pay the nominee.

LEGAL HEIR:
- Legal heirs are determined by succession law (Hindu Succession Act, Indian Succession Act, Muslim Personal Law, etc.).
- They are the rightful owners of the deceased's estate.
- If there is a WILL: The beneficiaries named in the will are the legal heirs.
- If there is NO WILL (intestate succession): Legal heirs are determined by applicable personal law.

HINDU SUCCESSION ACT — CLASS I HEIRS:
- Son, Daughter, Widow/Widower
- Mother
- Son of a pre-deceased son
- Daughter of a pre-deceased son
- Son of a pre-deceased daughter
- Daughter of a pre-deceased daughter
- Widow of a pre-deceased son
All Class I heirs have EQUAL share in the property.

LEGAL HEIR CERTIFICATE:
STEP 1: Apply at Tehsildar / Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) office
STEP 2: Submit:
  - Death certificate
  - Applicant's identity proof (Aadhaar)
  - Address proof
  - Relationship proof (ration card, family ID)
  - Affidavit sworn before a Notary/Magistrate listing all legal heirs
  - Two witnesses with their ID proofs
STEP 3: Verification by Revenue official (may include field inquiry)
STEP 4: Certificate issued within 15-30 days
- Cost: ₹50-200 (varies by state)
- Link (varies by state): Check e-District portal of your state

SUCCESSION CERTIFICATE:
- Issued by Civil Court under Indian Succession Act, Section 372
- Required for: Movable property (bank accounts, shares, FDs) when balance is high and no nominee exists
- More legally robust than Legal Heir Certificate

STEP 1: File petition in the Civil Court having jurisdiction over the deceased's last residence
STEP 2: Required documents:
  - Death certificate
  - List of assets/debts
  - Relationship proof
  - Details of all legal heirs
  - Affidavit
  - Court fee (generally 2-3% of asset value, varies by state)
STEP 3: Court publishes notice in newspaper (45 days waiting period for objections)
STEP 4: If no objections, court issues Succession Certificate
- Timeline: 2-6 months
- Cost: Court fees + lawyer fees + newspaper publication charges

WHEN IS WHAT NEEDED:
| Scenario | Document Needed |
|---|---|
| Bank account ≤ ₹5L, no nominee | Letter of Indemnity (no succession cert needed per RBI) |
| Bank account > ₹5L, no nominee | Legal Heir Certificate or Succession Certificate |
| Insurance claim, nominee exists | Nominee's claim form only |
| Insurance claim, no nominee | Legal Heir Certificate + consent of all heirs |
| Property transfer | Succession Certificate or Probate of Will |
| EPF claim, nominee exists | Form 20 with nominee details |
| EPF claim, no nominee | Legal Heir Certificate |
| Shares/Demat, no nominee | Succession Certificate (mandatory for SEBI-regulated assets) |

WILL AND PROBATE:
- If the deceased left a valid will: Probate may be required in some states (mandatory in Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai)
- Probate: Court-certified validation of a will
- Without probate (where not mandatory): Will + death certificate may suffice for many purposes
- Registered Will vs Unregistered Will: Registration is NOT mandatory but registered wills have stronger legal standing

IMPORTANT TIPS:
- Get Legal Heir Certificate FIRST — it's faster and cheaper than Succession Certificate
- Many institutions accept Legal Heir Certificate for amounts up to ₹5-10 lakh
- For larger amounts or disputed cases, Succession Certificate is more reliable
- If there's a dispute among heirs: Legal consultation recommended
- Keep multiple certified copies of whatever certificate you obtain
