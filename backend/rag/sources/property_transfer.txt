Property Transfer After Death (India):

OVERVIEW:
Transferring property (immovable assets) after death is one of the most complex post-death processes. It involves mutation of land/property records, which is handled by the Revenue Department or Municipal Corporation.

TYPES OF PROPERTY:
1. Self-acquired property: Governed by the will (if exists) or succession laws
2. Ancestral property: Governed by Hindu Succession Act or applicable personal law
3. Joint property: Survivorship rights may apply (depends on form of ownership)

SCENARIO A: With a Valid Will
STEP 1: Obtain Probate of Will (mandatory in Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai; optional elsewhere)
  - File petition in District Court
  - Submit: Original will, death certificate, list of heirs, court fee
  - Timeline: 3-12 months
STEP 2: Apply for Mutation at local Revenue/Municipal office
STEP 3: Submit documents (see checklist below)
STEP 4: Mutation recorded in property records
- Link: State Revenue Department portal

SCENARIO B: Without a Will (Intestate Succession)
STEP 1: Obtain Legal Heir Certificate or Succession Certificate
STEP 2: If multiple heirs exist and one wants sole ownership: Execute a Release Deed or Family Settlement Deed
STEP 3: Apply for Mutation at local Revenue/Municipal office
STEP 4: Submit documents (see checklist below)
STEP 5: Mutation recorded

STEP-BY-STEP MUTATION PROCESS:
STEP 1: Collect all property documents
  - Sale deed / title deed
  - Previous mutation records
  - Property tax receipts
  - Encumbrance certificate (from Sub-Registrar office)

STEP 2: Apply for Mutation
  - Urban property: Apply at Municipal Corporation / Nagar Palika
  - Rural property: Apply at Tehsildar / Revenue office
  - Online portals available in many states:
    - Maharashtra: https://mahabhulekh.maharashtra.gov.in/
    - Karnataka: https://landrecords.karnataka.gov.in/
    - Uttar Pradesh: https://upbhulekh.gov.in/
    - Tamil Nadu: https://eservices.tn.gov.in/

STEP 3: Submit Required Documents
  - Application form for mutation
  - Death certificate (certified copy)
  - Legal Heir Certificate / Succession Certificate / Probate
  - Original sale deed / title deed (for verification)
  - Property tax paid receipts (latest)
  - Encumbrance certificate (last 13-30 years)
  - NOC from other legal heirs (if applicable)
  - Release Deed / Family Settlement (if applicable)
  - Identity proof of applicant (Aadhaar + PAN)
  - Two passport-size photos

STEP 4: Verification
  - Revenue inspector may conduct field inspection
  - Notice may be published for objections (15-30 days)
  - Hearing scheduled if objections are raised

STEP 5: Mutation Order
  - Revenue officer passes mutation order
  - Property records updated in the name of legal heir(s)
  - Get certified copy of mutation record

ADDITIONAL STEPS:
- Update property tax records at Municipal Corporation
- Update electricity/water connection names
- Update Aadhaar address (if moving into property)
- Register the property transfer at Sub-Registrar office (stamp duty may apply)

STAMP DUTY AND REGISTRATION:
- Mutation by succession: Reduced stamp duty in most states
- Some states exempt stamp duty for transfers to spouse/children by succession
- Registration at Sub-Registrar: Typically needed for updating the sale deed
- Check state-specific stamp duty rates

HOUSING SOCIETY (Apartments):
- Inform the Housing Society in writing with death certificate
- Apply for transfer of share certificate
- Submit: Death certificate, Legal Heir Certificate, NOC from other heirs
- Society may charge a nominal transfer fee
- Update maintenance billing name

AGRICULTURAL LAND:
- Special rules apply in many states
- Some states restrict transfer to non-agriculturists
- Revenue records (7/12 extract in Maharashtra, Khata in Karnataka) must be updated
- Approach Tehsildar office

DISPUTES AND LITIGATION:
- If heirs disagree: Mediation recommended first
- If mediation fails: Civil suit for partition
- If illegal possession by others: File suit for possession in Civil Court
- Legal aid available for economically weaker sections

TIMELINE SUMMARY:
- Document collection: 2-4 weeks
- Legal Heir / Succession Certificate: 2 weeks to 6 months
- Mutation application & processing: 1-3 months
- Total: 3-9 months (varies significantly by state and complexity)

IMPORTANT NOTE:
- Property mutation does NOT transfer ownership by itself. It is an update of revenue records.
- Legal ownership transfer happens through Will/Succession, not mutation.
- Always consult a local property lawyer for specific cases.
