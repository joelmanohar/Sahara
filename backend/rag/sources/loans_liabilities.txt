Loans and Liabilities Settlement After Death (India):

OVERVIEW:
Debts and liabilities do not automatically disappear when a person dies. They must be settled from the deceased's estate. However, legal heirs are NOT personally liable for debts beyond the value of inherited assets (unless they are co-borrowers or guarantors).

KEY PRINCIPLE:
- Debts are settled from the deceased's estate (assets)
- Legal heirs are NOT personally liable (unless co-borrower/guarantor)
- Secured loans: Lender can seize the pledged asset
- Unsecured loans: Recovered from estate; cannot pursue heirs personally

TYPE 1: HOME LOAN / MORTGAGE

SCENARIO A: With Co-borrower
- Co-borrower continues to pay EMIs
- Transfer loan to co-borrower's name
- Submit death certificate to bank
- If co-borrower cannot pay: Sell property, settle loan, distribute remainder

SCENARIO B: Without Co-borrower — Insurance Exists
- Most home loans have credit life insurance
- File claim with insurance company
- Insurance settles the outstanding loan amount
- Property becomes debt-free for legal heirs
- Check if the deceased had PMJJBY (₹2 lakh cover) or bank's group insurance

SCENARIO C: Without Co-borrower — No Insurance
STEP 1: Inform the bank about the death
STEP 2: Submit death certificate and legal heir documents
STEP 3: Options:
  a) Legal heir continues EMI payments and retains property
  b) Bank sells property (with heir's consent), settles loan, returns surplus
  c) If property value < loan amount: Bank writes off the shortfall (heirs not liable for deficit)
STEP 4: Transfer property title to heir's name after loan settlement

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS:
- Death certificate
- Legal Heir Certificate / Succession Certificate
- Original loan agreement
- Property documents
- Insurance policy (if applicable)
- KYC of claimant/heir

TYPE 2: PERSONAL LOAN

- Personal loans are UNSECURED — no collateral
- If deceased had loan insurance: Insurance settles the loan
- If no insurance: Loan is recovered from the estate
- If no assets in estate: Bank writes off the loan
- Legal heirs CANNOT be forced to pay from their personal funds
- Exception: If heir is a co-borrower or guarantor, they are liable

IMPORTANT: If bank/recovery agents harass heirs for personal loan repayment:
- This is ILLEGAL (as per RBI Fair Practice Code)
- File complaint with Banking Ombudsman
  Link: https://cms.rbi.org.in/
- Report to police if harassment continues

TYPE 3: VEHICLE LOAN

- Vehicle loan is secured against the vehicle
- If loan insurance exists: Insurance settles remaining amount
- If no insurance:
  a) Heir continues EMIs and retains vehicle (transfer to heir's name)
  b) Surrender vehicle to lender
  c) Sell vehicle, settle loan, keep remainder
- Vehicle RC transfer also required (see RTO process)
- RTO: Submit death certificate + legal heir certificate + Form 31

TYPE 4: CREDIT CARD DEBT

- Credit card debt is UNSECURED
- Card issuer will attempt recovery from estate
- If no assets: Debt is written off
- Heirs are NOT liable unless they are supplementary card holders
- Inform the bank to cancel the credit card immediately
- Outstanding auto-debits/subscriptions will stop once card is cancelled

TYPE 5: GOLD LOAN

- Gold loan is secured against pledged gold
- Options:
  a) Pay outstanding amount and retrieve gold
  b) If unable to pay: Bank auctions the gold and settles the loan
  c) Surplus from auction returned to heir
- Submit death certificate and legal heir documents

TYPE 6: EDUCATION LOAN

- If deceased is the student (borrower): Co-borrower/guarantor is liable
- If deceased is the parent (co-borrower): Student continues payments
- Insurance (if any): Settles the loan
- Moratorium: Banks may grant temporary moratorium for hardship

TYPE 7: BUSINESS LOANS

- Sole proprietorship: Personal liability — settled from estate
- Partnership firm: Other partners may be jointly liable
- Private Limited Company: Company's liability, not personal
- Directors: Only liable if they gave personal guarantees

GENERAL STEPS FOR ALL LOANS:

STEP 1: Inform all lenders about the death (within 1-2 weeks)
STEP 2: Submit death certificate to each lender
STEP 3: Request outstanding balance statement
STEP 4: Check for loan insurance / credit life insurance
STEP 5: If insured: File insurance claim to settle the loan
STEP 6: If uninsured: Negotiate with lender for settlement/closure
STEP 7: Obtain No Dues Certificate (NDC) after settlement
STEP 8: Keep NDC safely — it protects heirs from future claims

CREDIT REPORT:
- CIBIL record of the deceased should be updated
- Submit death certificate to CIBIL/TransUnion
  Link: https://www.cibil.com/
- Prevents identity theft and fraudulent applications

GOVERNMENT SCHEMES:
- PMJJBY (Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana): ₹2 lakh life cover, ₹436/year premium
  - If deceased was enrolled: File claim with bank
  - Link: https://www.jansuraksha.gov.in/
- PMSBY (Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana): ₹2 lakh accidental death cover
  - File claim at the bank where premium was debited
  - Link: https://www.jansuraksha.gov.in/

ESCALATION:
- Bank harassment: Banking Ombudsman (https://cms.rbi.org.in/)
- NBFC harassment: RBI NBFC complaint
- Recovery agent misconduct: Police complaint + RBI complaint
- Legal recourse: Consumer Court for unfair bank practices

IMPORTANT NOTES:
- Never sign any document acknowledging personal liability for the deceased's debts
- Recovery agents cannot force entry, seize property without court order, or harass
- Joint account debits after death: Inform bank immediately to freeze the deceased's share
- Get legal advice if debt amounts are large or recovery pressure is intense
