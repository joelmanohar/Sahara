Bank Account Claims After Death (India — RBI Guidelines):

OVERVIEW:
When an account holder passes away, the bank account must be either transferred to the nominee/legal heir or closed. The process depends on whether a nominee exists, the account balance, and the type of account.

SCENARIO A: Nominee Exists
STEP 1: Visit the bank branch where the account is held
STEP 2: Inform the branch manager about the death and request the "Claim Form for Deceased Account Holder"
STEP 3: Submit the following documents:
  - Death certificate (certified copy)
  - Nominee's identity proof (Aadhaar + PAN)
  - Nominee's bank account details (for fund transfer)
  - Original passbook or last statement
  - Duly filled claim/nomination form
STEP 4: Bank verifies documents (7-15 working days)
STEP 5: Funds transferred to nominee's account or demand draft issued
- Timeline: 15-30 working days
- Link: https://www.rbi.org.in/

SCENARIO B: No Nominee — Balance ≤ ₹5,00,000
Per RBI Master Circular, banks MUST settle claims without insisting on succession certificate:
STEP 1: Visit the branch and explain the situation
STEP 2: Submit:
  - Death certificate (certified copy)
  - Letter of Indemnity (provided by bank)
  - Affidavit on stamp paper (as per bank format)
  - Claimant's KYC (Aadhaar + PAN)
  - Relationship proof (ration card, family ID, or affidavit)
STEP 3: Bank may ask for surety from another account holder
STEP 4: Settlement within 15-30 working days
- Important: RBI mandates that banks should NOT insist on legal heir certificate or succession certificate for amounts up to ₹5 lakh.

SCENARIO C: No Nominee — Balance > ₹5,00,000
STEP 1: Obtain Legal Heir Certificate from Tehsildar/SDM OR Succession Certificate from Civil Court
  - Legal Heir Certificate: Apply at Tehsildar office with death certificate, family details, and two witnesses. Processing: 15-30 days. Cost: ₹50-100.
  - Succession Certificate: Apply in Civil Court under Indian Succession Act. Processing: 2-6 months. Cost: Court fees + lawyer fees.
STEP 2: Submit to bank along with:
  - Death certificate
  - Claimant's KYC
  - Bank claim form
  - Original passbook
STEP 3: Bank processes claim (15-30 working days after document verification)

JOINT ACCOUNT:
- If account is "Either or Survivor" or "Former or Survivor": The surviving holder can continue operating the account. Submit death certificate to update records.
- If account is "Joint": The surviving holder(s) need to follow the claim process above.

FIXED DEPOSITS (FD):
- Nominee exists: FD can be prematurely withdrawn or transferred. No penalty for premature closure due to death.
- No nominee: Same process as Scenario B or C above.

RECURRING DEPOSITS (RD):
- Can be prematurely closed with death certificate + nominee/legal heir documents.
- Matured RDs: Claim directly with documents.

SAFE DEPOSIT LOCKER:
- Nominee/legal heir can access the locker in the presence of bank officials.
- Contents inventoried and handed over.
- Locker rent must be settled.

ESCALATION:
- If bank delays beyond 30 days: File complaint with Banking Ombudsman
  Link: https://cms.rbi.org.in/
- RBI Helpline: 14448
- Written complaint to Regional Office of RBI

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS CHECKLIST:
1. Death certificate (certified copy) × 2
2. Claimant's Aadhaar card
3. Claimant's PAN card
4. Bank account passbook or last statement
5. Claim form (bank-specific)
6. Letter of Indemnity (if no nominee, balance ≤ ₹5L)
7. Legal Heir Certificate / Succession Certificate (if no nominee, balance > ₹5L)
8. Relationship proof
9. Cancelled cheque of claimant's account (for fund transfer)
